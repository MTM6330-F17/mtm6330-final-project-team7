Program Overview

This two-year Ontario College Diploma program is meant to prepare students for a variety of jobs in the digital media industry. Students will learn computer programming skills such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, PHP, SASS, jQuerry, mySQL and CMS. Students will explore visual design and learn how to use Photoshop, Illustrator, Lightroom, Premiere, After Effects and Audition. Classes like Digital Citizenship, User Experience Design and Project Management Skills, will provide students with great knowledge of UX Design, dealing with clients and time management. Finally, Classes like Introduction to DSLR and Video Foundations, will provide students with knowledge on how to use a DSLR camera. At level 4, students will be given a choice of going into the computer programming side of the industry, or on the visual design side. Students will work alone, and in teams. They will also work with live clients and gain practical experience. The curriculum is delivered on a variety of platforms including Apple OS X, Windows and a variety of mobile operating systems.

Design Foundations

Through a hands-on approach to design theory, students learn to be objective rather than subjective about good design. Students are introduced to fundamental design rules, including principles in layout, typography, colour theory, user interfaces and interaction design. Modern industry-standard software is used to explore different design styles, methodologies and best practices.

Videography

Students learn the full video production life cycle, from planning and pre-production, to editing and post-production. Video and audio technology and tools are introduced, building on the techniques and best practices developed through DSLR photography. Students practise working through pre-production and production of both video and audio, ensuring the best quality result when transitioning to post-production to limit the amount of correction needed during the editing process. Students learn to use industry tools to create final projects.

Motion Graphics

Motion graphics will teach students how to use Adobe After Effects. Using this software, students will learn how to make animations, enhance existing video footage and create special effects. Students work with multiple forms of media, syncing together audio, video, compositions, transitions and added effects to create professional quality video.
